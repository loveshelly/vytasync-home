FAQ

Everything you
need to know.

What is cervical traction, and is it safe?
+
Cervical traction is a well-established physiotherapy technique that gently elongates the cervical spine to relieve compressed discs and tight muscles. Vytasync replicates this through patented algorithm-controlled air chambers. The pressure is gradual and fully adjustable across 5 intensity levels — designed to feel relaxing rather than mechanical.
How does the adaptive height work?
+
Dual independent air chambers adjust pressure in real time based on your sleeping position. Whether you're on your back, side, or switching frequently, the system redistributes support automatically. You can also manually fine-tune your preferred height range through the app or the bedside control unit.
Can it help with snoring?
+
Consistent cervical alignment helps maintain a naturally open airway during sleep — reducing the micro-collapses responsible for snoring and shallow breathing. The breath regulation mode also guides your nervous system into a calmer state before and during sleep. Note: Vytasync is not a medical device and results vary by individual.
Is it noisy? I'm a light sleeper.
+
Vytasync 2.0 operates below 20 decibels — quieter than a whisper. The soft-membrane air chambers and brushless micro-pump are engineered to be inaudible during normal sleep. Most users report they are completely unaware the system is running.
What sleeping positions does it support?
+
Vytasync 2.0 is designed for back sleepers, side sleepers, and combination sleepers. The dual independent chambers adjust for each positional need automatically throughout the night.
